Many practice it, few question it

Communion is something many believers practice, but not everyone stops to ask: Where did it really come from?

Some take it as routine. Others see it as sacred. But understanding begins when we return to the origin.

It began with Jesus

Communion started with Jesus at the Last Supper. He sat with His disciples, took bread, gave thanks, broke it, and shared it. Then He took the cup and gave it to them.

In that moment, He gave a clear instruction: “Do this in remembrance of Me.”

Key Message
Communion is rooted in Christ, not created by tradition.

The meaning is simple but powerful

The bread represents His body. The cup represents His blood.

Together, they remind us of His sacrifice, His love, and the price He paid for us.

Tradition shaped the method

Over time, churches developed different ways of practicing communion. Some serve it weekly, some monthly. Some use wine, others grape juice.

These differences show that tradition has influenced the method, but it should never replace the meaning.

Meaning must remain

Communion is not about performance. It is not about appearance.

It is about remembrance. It is about examining the heart. It is about staying connected to Christ.

Routine or relationship?

When communion becomes routine without understanding, its power is reduced.

But when taken with truth, humility, and awareness, even the simplest moment becomes deeply meaningful.

📖 Scripture Anchor
Luke 22:19
“Do this in remembrance of Me.”
— The Bible
