Storms can demand our attention

Storms have a way of pulling our eyes toward fear, uncertainty, and what feels out of control. In the middle of noise, it can be hard to believe that God is still close. But loud moments do not mean God is distant.

Jesus was already moving toward them

In the story of the disciples on the water, the storm was real. The waves were strong. The danger was present. Yet Jesus was not far — He was already moving toward them.

Key Message
God’s nearness is not weakened by storms.

Noise can shift our focus

Fear grew because the disciples focused on what surrounded them, not on who was with them. Noise has power when it becomes the center of our vision. It convinces us that we are alone, even when help is near.

Silence sees the storm differently

For Deaf individuals, silence changes how storms are experienced. Without sound, attention is guided by sight and awareness rather than noise. The storm may still exist, but it does not dominate the heart in the same way.

God enters the storm with us

God’s presence is not measured by calm conditions. He does not wait for the storm to end before drawing near. He enters the storm with us.

Presence comes before peace

Jesus spoke reassurance, but His presence came first. Before the wind settled, fear was addressed. Before the waves stopped, peace was offered.

God is closer than we realize

When the world feels loud, God is often closer than we realize. He is not absent in the storm. He is near, steady, and present.
